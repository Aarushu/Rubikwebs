About


The Rubik's cube was invented in 1974 by Professor Erno Rubik in Budapest, Hungary.


The Rubik's Cube Was Meant To Be A Classroom Teaching Tool But His Students Thought That It Was Pretty Cool.


On June 5, 1982, the first world championship was held in Budapest, Hungary. 19 people competed in the event and the American Minh Thai won with a single solve time of 22.95 seconds and was considered as the First World Record of the Rubik's Cube.

How I Got Into Cubing

So, This Is How I Got Into Cubing.


It Was One Fine Day When I Went To My Cousin Brothers House. I'm 6 Years Older Than Him. When I Entered, The First Thin I saw Was The Cube. I was Fascinated. I Had A Couple Of Cubes In The Past, But I Used To Peel Off The Stickers. So When I Tried To Solve It, I Was Really Confused. I Could Make One Side, But The Others Were Difficult. I Then Aske My Mom To Get One Last Cube And I would Learn How To Solve It. And, As Soon As I Got It, I Was Happy. It Took Me A Week Of Hard Practice And I Learned How To Solve it. My Solve Time At That Time Was 2-4 Minutes. When My Mom Saw That I Had Learned How To Solve It, She Was Over The Moon. Then That Was When I got My First SpeedCube. The Cubelelo Drift Warrior 3*3 Speedcube. It Is The Best Cube I Have Used And I have Made My Solve Time Under 1 Minute.
